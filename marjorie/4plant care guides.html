Plant care isn’t always the easiest when you’re unsure of where to start. You'll notice all the amazing advantages of being a plant parent once you understand the basics and establish a routine. Plants have been proved to improve your breathing and mood simply by being around. We've highlighted some excellent plant care ideas to assist you in taking care of your new plant babies, whether you're decorating your room or office with houseplants. Following these plant-care guidelines will become second nature to you in no time!


1. Feel the Soil to Know How Often to Water Your Plants
You may be wondering how often to water your potted plants at home. The golden rule for most plants is to check if the first inch or so of soil is dry. If the soil is dry, the plant has to be watered. If the plant's leaves have shriveled or become dry or discolored, it may require a little more water than usual.


2. It’s Better to Underwater Your Plants Than Overwater Them
When a plant is deprived of water, it recovers faster than when it is given too much. Before transplanting an overwatered plant into a new container, you may need to repot the plant and remove any unhealthy roots and overwatered soil.


3. Skip Fertilization for Houseplants if you’re Unsure
Unless your houseplant is failing to develop, you don't need to fertilize it. It's best to forego fertilizer altogether if you're unsure of how much or what type to use when planting. If you fertilize your plant too much, it may die rather than thrive.


4. Houseplants Love Stability
Before choosing a plant, make sure you know where you'll put it in your home. Plants grow once they've been accustomed to their surroundings, so obtaining the proper quantity of light is crucial. Temperature plays a role as well. Fluctuating temperatures may shock your plant, preventing it from developing and perhaps causing it to die. Temperatures between 65 and 75 degrees Fahrenheit are ideal for most plants.


5. Smaller Plants Are the Fastest Growing Plants
It is usually preferable to purchase a smaller plant over a larger one when purchasing your chosen plant. This is because a smaller plant will be able to establish itself in its environment more quickly and have a higher root-to-top growth ratio. The roots of a larger plant will not be able to keep up with the top growth until the roots catch up.
