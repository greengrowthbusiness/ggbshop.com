Even if you just have a little yard or outdoor living space, you can still have a beautiful garden. There are several ways to turn a tiny space into a huge garden area with little effort and, in most cases, no money out of pocket. Whether you live in an apartment with only tarmac for a yard or have a little yard that doesn't seem big enough for a garden, we've compiled a list of fantastic small garden ideas for you to try this spring.
With spring and planting season just around the corner, now is the perfect time to pick a small-space garden design and get started. Many of these suggestions are excellent even if you have a large garden but want something a little closer to your home. Whether you want to grow veggies, flowers, or create your own herb garden, this large variety is guaranteed to offer a garden concept for your little space.


1. Repurposed Furniture Garden
With some old furniture that you were preparing to toss out, you may create a lovely outdoor space. Planting bases can be made from of old dressers. Simply place the plants in individual pots and store them in the drawers. You'll end up with a three-tiered garden that's both attractive and practical. If you don't already have an old dresser, you can probably get one for a few bucks at a yard sale or flea market, and then all you have to do is start planting. Recycled Upside-Down Garden Recycling is a cost-effective and space-saving solution. Apartment dwellers will love this repurposed upside down garden idea, or you can simply hang your plants on your porch if you have one. You only need to select the containers you wish to use and then follow the directions for hanging your plants correctly so that they grow as they should. This is a terrific way to display flowers, herbs, and various vegetables while also saving space.


2.  Vertical Garden
Building a vertical garden with a shoe organizer is a brilliant idea. This garden not only looks amazing and saves space, but it also keeps pests and pets away from your herbs and vegetables. All you have to do now is fill each shoe space with potting soil or compost before adding your plants. Make sure your hanging gets enough sunlight for the plants, and if there is no cover from the rain overhead, you will need to water them on a regular basis.


3. Hanging Gutter Garden
A stunning hanging garden may be made out of old guttering. The amount of guttering you'll need is determined by the size of the garden you're planning. Gutter gardens make use of the vertical space in your yard, allowing you to grow flowers, herbs, and veggies even if you don't have much of a grass. Just make sure you pick a location that receives a few hours of direct sunshine each day. Gutter gardens can also act as a natural privacy fence or divider in your garden.


4. Kitchen Fairy Garden
You don't have to employ fairies in this garden, but the entire concept is fantastic for small places. A container is required. An ancient wooden barrel serves as the planting's foundation in this instance. Once you've got a container, all you have to do now is start filling it up with dirt and smaller containers. In this garden, you'll see a muffin tin that's ideal for smaller herbs. You could also use ordinary terra cotta pots or any other container. If you want to achieve a kitchen look, old pots and pans are ideal.


5. Vertical Pallet Garden
An old pallet, or several, can be transformed into the ideal planter for vertical gardens. Vertical planters are fantastic even if you have a vast outdoor space because they conserve space and are quite appealing to look at. They can be used to expand your current garden area, allowing you to plant more flowers, herbs, or vegetables. All you have to do now is secure your terra cotta pots to the pallets using zip ties or something similar, and then decide what to plant.


6. Kiddie Pool Garden
If you don't want to rip up your lawn or don't have one, an old plastic kiddie pool makes an excellent raised garden bed. You might go in and cover the pool with stones or bricks if you want to make it more appealing. All you have to do now is fill the pool with soil or compost and start planting. This is advantageous since you may remove the pool over the winter if desired, and then reinstall it when planting season arrives.


7. Portable Container Garden
Container gardens are convenient since they can be moved around. You may move them if you need to, and you won't have to worry about grass regrowing in your garden. A colorful container garden, whether you have a fence or deck, is a terrific way to add a little beauty to the area while also saving space for your planting. Choose colorful bucket planters and hang them on your fence or from windowsills and other locations around the house.


8. Tiered Garden
For tiny places, tiered gardens are ideal. If you just have a small amount of area for flowers or vegetables, a few terra cotta pots can be used to create a beautiful tiered garden. You can plant whatever you want in the planters once they're piled, and depending on how many planters you use, you can have as many plants as you like. If you want, you can use plastic planters, but the terra cotta ones are more durable and will last much longer. This is a terrific yearly garden idea, especially if you want something colorful on your porch.


9. Garden Bench Planters
The addition of benches and other decorations to small garden spaces should not be a problem. In fact, you could add a lovely bench and then use it as a plant stand. This is an excellent solution for outdated seats that you can no longer sit on. Simply use the seat to hold your planters, or get very creative and use a bench without a seat by suspending the plants or making a raised garden from the seat space.


10. Hanging Tin Can Garden
You can recycle old tin cans and put them in your garden. You may just paint some tin cans and use them as planters if you don't have enough space to grow all of the flowers, herbs, or vegetables you want. These look great hung from fences, decks, or even the side of a house. Painted tin cans can be used to construct a full garden on the side of a fence, and they are portable so you can move them about as needed.


11. Wooden Fence Planter Boxes
Simply hang planter boxes from your fence to create a garden area. You merely need to fill terra cotta planters with your plants once the boxes are in place. This is a great small garden idea for those of you who don't have much of a yard but yet want to grow fresh herbs, vegetables, and flowers. Different planters can be used to add color, and the boxes are simple to create out of old wood pieces.


12. Vertical Garden Tower
This vertical tower is a great addition to even the tiniest garden spaces. It's simple to construct, and once it's finished, simply add the plants you desire, which will all be contained within the vertical framework. If you want to add more plants, make it taller, or make it shorter and add a few to your garden area. It's a lot easy than it appears, and you'll save a lot of money over buying one that's already built.


13. Elevated Gardens
Elevated garden boxes can be built and used throughout your landscaping. If you don't have much of a yard and want to maintain your garden on the deck or porch, these are ideal. Simply construct the boxes to the desired height. It's worth noting that you don't have to design them with legs. Elevated garden boxes are popular and can be used to decorate steps, decks, and other tiny garden areas. To give depth to your garden space, you can build stackable boxes or make them in different sizes.


14. Vertical Soda Bottle Garden
This vertical soda bottle garden is a terrific way to display plants or cultivate vegetables on fences. You also get the extra benefit of being able to recycle and reuse those 2-liter beverage bottles. The garden's essence is really straightforward. Simply cut the edge of the bottles off and plant whatever you wish in the empty hole. This is a fantastic herb garden idea. You can hang the bottles on the side of the house, directly outside the kitchen door, to make it easy to get to your herbs.


15. Patio Umbrella Garden
You can repurpose a large planter and use it as a holder for your patio umbrella if you have a small patio and want to incorporate some green. You'll only need pipes to hold the umbrella, and you'll need to decide the plants or flowers you'd like to put next to you while sitting outside. You could even get really creative and use a tiered planter to add a few more plants and create a mini flower garden right next to your seats.


16. Garden Fountain
Smaller gardens benefit from fountains. They are not only simple to construct, but they also lend dimension to any garden, making it appear larger. A fountain can be made out of a variety of materials, and depending on how you make it, you might be able to include some plants in the design. This combines the soothing sound of water with the lushness of gorgeous flowers or plants to create a soothing environment. For people who don't have much yard to plant, some fountain designs may be great for decks or patios.


17. Suspended String Garden
This is a cute way to cultivate those plants, and it's ideal for little spaces. These suspended string containers are a terrific option if you don't have enough space for a garden or just want to add a little flair to your porch or patio. Instead of worrying about land, you may just make use of the space above you. These would also look fantastic hanging from trellises or other structures in or near your garden.


18. Pathway Gardening
You may still have a great route even if you don't have a lot of room, and alongside that pathway is the ideal spot for planting. Outlining a garden path with small shrubs, hostas, and other leafy plants looks lovely. You may design a path through your yard or a small garden out of any number of materials, and you can conserve space and make your path appear even more lovely by using the path's outline for planting. If you already have a path – whether it leads to the garden or to the sidewalk – use the space beside it to grow plants and flowers.


19. Cinder Wall Garden
With cinder blocks, you can create a beautiful garden wall that holds numerous plants. This is perfect for smaller yards and can be built right next to the fence. You can toggle the blocks however you want to create different planting areas and to make the entire unit unique.


20. Balcony Gardens
Even if you only have a balcony or a tiny deck, you can still create a lush garden space that is ideal for relaxing during the summer. You can use full, mature plants for your balcony garden, contrary to popular belief. You'll need to plant in containers, but there's no end to the beauty you can add to your balcony, and mixing larger plants with smaller ones creates a lovely garden area that takes up far less space than you might think.


21. Mix Plant Gardens
You can mix the plants in each container to make them more colorful and give them depth if you're growing in containers. This gives the impression of a much larger garden while just taking up a little amount of space. To acquire more beauty per square inch, combine hanging foliage with flowers. Many diverse plants grow nicely together and look lovely in containers when mixed together. Plus, even if you only have a few pots to deal with, you get to use as many flowers and greenery as you want.
